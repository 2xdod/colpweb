The Code of Noble Honor

In the grand manors of Colp Town's Noble Quarter, where diplomacy and intrigue dance together at every social gathering, Lord Chancellor Reginald Blackwood recognized the need for accessories that would help distinguish true gentlemen from mere pretenders. These tie pins were his solution to a growing problem of duplicity in high society.

Each pin is blessed with the ancient magic of truth-seeing, allowing the wearer to sense when others speak with dishonest intentions. The crest itself grows warm when in the presence of falsehood, providing a subtle but invaluable advantage in negotiations and social interactions. Only those who wear it with genuine honor can access its protective powers.
